## 📖 Overview

Sequoia Capital partner Julien Bek said it plainly: **"The next trillion-dollar company will look like a services company — but run like a software company."**

SaaS is not dead. It is splitting apart.

The seat-based pricing model has begun to collapse. Klarna replaced 700 customer service agents with AI equivalent to 25 people. Salesforce launched "Agentforce," questioning the very existence of its own SaaS products. $2 trillion in market capitalization has evaporated from the SaaS sector, and the world where 500-seat licenses compress to 50 has already begun.

But the real structural shift lies beyond.

The software market is $1 trillion. The services market is $6 trillion. When AI shifts from selling "tools" to selling "completed work," the TAM expands sixfold. Sierra ($10B valuation), Harvey ($11B valuation), Mercor — pioneers embodying this structural transformation have already emerged.

**This book is not a death certificate for SaaS. It is a blueprint for how AI rewrites industrial structure.**

Drawing on primary sources from Sequoia Capital, BCG, Gartner, and McKinsey, alongside implementation cases from pioneering companies worldwide, this book structures the conditions that the "next trillion-dollar company" must satisfy.
